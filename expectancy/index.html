Manager Wins Above Expectancy

Wins and championships simply tell us that a manager had a lot of talent on his team. If he has a lot of talent, he is supposed to win. But how about when a talented team doesn’t win? What about when a team wins more than it should, based on its runs scored and runs allowed? How much is actually luck? If it is all luck, wouldn’t it even out over thousands of games? Is there more than luck involved? Is the manager responsible for some of this over- or under-performance?

This table looks at Win Expectancy for a manager based on two factors—pythagorean record and combined wins above replacement for the players on the team. For more, read the post at Beyond the Box Score. The columns in this table are:

  • ExpW/pyth: Expected Wins, according to pythagorean record
  • WAE/pyth: Wins Above Expectancy, according to pythagorean record (positive means manager over-performed expectation)
  • ExpW/war: Expected Wins, according to WAR
  • WAE/war: Wins Above Expectancy, according to WAR (positive means manager over-performed expectation)
  • TotalWAE: Sum of both Wins Above Expectancy totals
